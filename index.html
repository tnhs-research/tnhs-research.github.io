Understanding ICT Students' Behavior on Mobile Legends: Bang Bang in Taligaman National High School James E. Rosales, Jane C. Rosales, John Ray S. Subrastas 2025

This study explores the behavioral patterns of ICT students in relation to Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, investigating playing frequency, motivations, and impact on academic performance. Findings suggest the game is a source of entertainment and social bonding, but calls for digital wellness education.

Keywords: Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, ICT students, gaming behavior, academic impact, digital wellness

Abstract: This study explores the behavioral patterns of ICT students at Taligaman National High School in relation to their engagement with the popular mobile game Mobile Legends: Bang Bang. Using a mixed-methods research design, the study investigates studentsâ€™ playing frequency, motivations, in-game spending habits, social interactions, and the potential impact of gameplay on their academic performance and daily routines. Data were gathered through survey questionnaires, focus group discussions, and informal interviews. Findings reveal that most ICT students view the game as a primary source of entertainment and social bonding, though excessive gameplay is associated with reduced study time and occasional classroom distractions. The study highlights the need for balanced gaming habits and recommends the integration of digital wellness education to promote responsible gaming behavior among students.

Digital Literacy Levels and Challenges Among Junior High School Teachers Dr. Amelia R. Diaz 2023

Assessing the digital literacy competency of JHS teachers, identifying key challenges in technology integration, and proposing targeted professional development interventions to enhance instructional strategies.

Keywords: Digital Literacy, JHS Teachers, Technology Integration, Professional Development, Educational Technology

Abstract: This survey-based research utilized a descriptive approach to measure the current digital literacy levels among Junior High School teachers at TNHS. Results indicated high proficiency in basic computing but moderate competency in advanced tools for instruction and assessment. The primary challenge identified was lack of time for continuous learning and outdated equipment. The study concludes with a recommendation for a mandatory, incentivized technology skills enhancement program focusing on cloud tools and AI literacy.
